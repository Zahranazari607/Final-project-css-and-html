Hello!

I'm Zahra Nazari — a general physician, independent researcher, and graphic designer with a deep passion for art, humanity, and truth.

I completed my medical education and have been working for several years in patient care and health promotion. Yet, my curious and creative mind has never been content with the world of medicine alone. Art is my second language, and design is my way of expressing emotions, perspectives, and creating a more beautiful world.

I often use a blend of pink, blue, and purple in my visual work to evoke calmness, energy, and hope. For me, colors are not just tools — they are the language of feelings. I value detail and structure deeply — whether it’s in crafting a simple and intuitive menu or selecting fonts that reflect my vibrant yet delicate personality.

Innovation and aesthetics are the core of my design philosophy. I strive to merge technology with artistic spirit, allowing each project to be a visual narrative of creativity, precision, and inspiration. Design is not just a profession for me — it is a way to breathe in a world that sometimes feels devoid of meaning.

Alongside my design work, I am an independent researcher with a strong interest in scientific writing, data analysis, and seeking answers to complex questions. My research has primarily focused on mental health, sleep quality, rare diseases, and patient experiences within challenging healthcare systems.

I live and work in Afghanistan — a country facing immense challenges, yet home to people with big hearts. I believe that in this land, both doctors and artists can be bearers of light, awareness, and hope. I've tried, even in silence, to play a small part in that mission.

If I were to describe myself in a few words:
Creative, detail-oriented, color-loving, inspired — and always seeking to bridge the worlds of science and art.
